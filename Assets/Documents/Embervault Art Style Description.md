# Embervault Art Style Description

Embervault's visual identity is defined by a cinematic, industrial aesthetic called Cinematic Vector Noir. It blends hard-edged geometric silhouettes with a warm, ember-driven color language to create a world that feels forged in metal and lit by dying fire.

The style uses a dark brown-black foundation to anchor the world in heavy shadow, while muted reddish-brown structures give the environment a grounded, industrial weight. Metallic gray surfaces act as reflective planes that catch and amplify the glow of the world, creating sharp, deliberate highlights.

The signature element is the interplay between red-orange ambient light and bright red energy accents. These glowing lines, cores, and extraction beams cut through the darkness like sparks in a furnace, giving the world a sense of heat, pressure, and contained volatility. Hard shadows and crisp silhouettes reinforce the graphic, vector-inspired look, while subtle edge highlights in light gray separate forms without breaking the mood.

Overall, the style feels like industrial geometry illuminated by embers: a fusion of minimalism, metal, and cinematic tension. It is instantly recognizable, visually bold, and perfectly suited to fast-paced extraction gameplay.

## Visual Direction Summary

- Aesthetic: Cinematic Vector Noir
- Core Mood: Industrial tension, heat, pressure, controlled danger
- Primary Contrast: Deep shadows vs ember-red energy accents
- Material Language: Matte industrial geometry with selective metallic highlights
- Readability Goal: Strong silhouettes and clear gameplay-relevant light cues

## Palette Guide (Practical)

- Foundation darks: brown-black, charcoal, near-black
- Structural mids: muted reddish-browns, desaturated rust tones
- Metals: neutral-to-cool grays for edges and hard surfaces
- Energy accents: red-orange ambient, bright red focal glows
- Highlight restraint: thin, intentional light-gray edge separation only where needed

## Lighting Rules

- Keep global lighting low-key and directional.
- Use red energy elements as the primary focal lights.
- Avoid overfilling shadows; maintain hard shape readability.
- Let extraction points, enemy cores, and weapon effects carry high-intensity red values.

## Shape and Composition Rules

- Prefer blocky, hard-edged silhouettes over organic forms.
- Build environments from layered geometric masses.
- Maintain clean figure-ground separation in combat spaces.
- Reserve high-contrast accents for interactables and threats.

## VFX and UI Alignment

- VFX should feel like sparks, vents, pulses, and contained furnace pressure.
- Keep effects short, sharp, and directional rather than smoky or diffuse.
- UI should inherit the same palette logic: dark base, ember highlights, strong contrast.
